Catching Light

The Faces of Marawi

Published November 2019
Scroll Down

On May 23, 2017, terrorist affiliates of the Islamic State laid siege to the Islamic City of Marawi, the provincial capital of Lanao del Sur and one of the most important urban centers in Muslim Mindanao. The terrorists spared none in their conquest of the city, killing wantonly and taking freely from the residents. Christians and Muslims alike were the victims of their cruel and inhumane actions.

But gradually, after months of fighting between government forces and the insurgents, Secretary of Defense Delfin Lorenzana declared the end of combat operations on October 23, 2017, and the rehabilitation program could begin in earnest.

The GUIDON looks into the situation in Marawi a year since the fated attack. The stories presented in this hub are the lived experiences of people affected by the siege and those trying to build back better from the carnage.
